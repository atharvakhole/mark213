Introduction

The oral care industry has seen a great increase in sales and revenue in recent years, owing to the increasing awareness among consumers. Consumers in developing countries have started adopting better oral health practices, contributing further to the oral care market.

This market research project is a study of the interdental cleaning market. Specifically, the research focuses on Oral-B, a pioneer and leader within the industry. The project looks into Oral-Bâ€™s range of Interdental Brushes, specifically tailored for the Singapore market.

The Oral Care Market

The world oral care market in 2020 was valued at $53 billion USD, and is estimated to reach $96 billion by the year 2031.

The Asia Pacific region is estimated to have the largest potential growth in the coming years where countries in the low-income bracket are shifting to the mid-income bracket and those in the mid-income bracket climbing into the high-income bracket.1

The Asia Pacific region dominated the market in 2021 with a revenue share of over 40.0%.2

As of 2020, tootbrushes account for $35,184.3 million in sales globally.

Interdental Brushes

When it comes to oral care, preventive measures play a key role in maintaining good dental health. This is where interdental brushes have emerged as a key player in order to prevent gum disease and tooth decay.

The importance of interdental cleaning cannot be overstated. It serves as a crucial element in removing debris and plaque from the hard-to-reach spaces between teeth, mitigating the risk of oral health problems.

Oral-B

At the forefront of this dental revolution is Oral-B. Founded in 1950 in California USA, Oral-B has been the industry pioneer for toothbrush technology, electric toothbrushes and other oral hygiene products.

Oral-B holds 9.7% of Brand market share worldwide as of 2022, which is the 2nd largest share after Colgate (14.9%)3. Oral-B and Crest are sister brands under the same corporate umbrella Procter & Gamble Co, and together they occupy 17.3% of the market share. Based on this we can infer that Oral-B is one of the largest players in the market worldwide.

Oral-B also holds 17.7% of the market share in Singapore, making it the largest brand in Singapore.4

When it comes to Oral-Care Appliances, Oral-B occupies a whopping 43.9% market share in Singapore, leading all competitors by a serious margin5.

In the pages that follow, we will explore the intricate details of the market environment surrounding Oral-B, the innovations that have shaped its product portfolio, and the unique characteristics of interdental brushes that cater to the diverse needs of the Singaporean market.
