Market Environment

Analysis of the Market for Interdental Toothbrushes

The market for interdental toothbrushes has exhibited significant growth, with a value of USD 267.55 million in 2023.

Despite being a fraction of the overall oral care market, the interdental brush segment is poised for substantial expansion, with a projected Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of approximately 7.25% over the next decade. By 2032, the market is estimated to reach a value of USD 410.11 million.1

This growth is driven by increasing awareness of oral health and the efficacy of interdental cleaning in preventing dental issues.

The global interdental cleaning product market size was USD 2.57 Billion in 2022 and is expected to reach USD 4.27 Billion in 2032

Key Macro Environmental Factors

Sociocultural Factors

  • Demographics emerge as a primary influencer in the interdental toothbrush market.
  • Consumers with higher education are more likely to invest in interdental brushes, driven by a heightened awareness of oral health issues.
  • Additionally, an aging population both globally and in Singapore contributes to the rising demand for effective oral care solutions.
  • The ease of use and effectiveness of interdental toothbrushes position them as an ideal solution for both younger and older demographics facing dental problems.

Economic Factors

  • Consumers in this region have higher disposable income which increases the likelihood of adoption

Political Factors

  • Singapore government initiatives such as the Zero Waste Masterplan are political considerations for Interdental Products.

    Getting businesses on board to support Singapore’s drive to reduce waste and operate sustainably is best done by an organisation that understands their needs and constraints. Since 2001, this role has been played by the Waste Management and Recycling Association of Singapore (WMRAS) – the national trade body for local waste disposal and resource recovery companies.

  • While compliance with sustainability policies might be a challenge, it also presesnts opportunities to lead the market in environment-friendly products
  • Interdental brushes have reusable components which can be leveraged in this regard

Legal Factors

  • There is a considerable amount of legislation and regulation regarding health care products including specific regulation for dental products
  • The Health Products (Oral Dental Gums) Regulations 2016 which is part of the Health Products Act provides a set of guidelines for this market
  • This includes regulation for Product Presentation and Advertisement, which will be key considerations when promoting products

Customer Segments and Target Customers

The target customer base for interdental toothbrushes is diverse, encompassing individuals aged 12 and above as recommended by healthcare professionals. Younger demographics, as well as older individuals with specific oral care needs, form a significant portion of the target market. With versatility in usage and a recommendation from the healthcare industry, the target customer range is extensive, spanning various demographics and lifestyles.
